# Ship the Letters

Tonight the Letters project ships. Tomorrow morning the anthology assembles and we critique, so this one's deadline is real: **live URL by the start of Day 9. Time-box: 90 minutes.**

## The checklist

Your site is done when:

- [ ] **Two pages**: the letter you typeset, and your reply, both rendered through your `LetterLayout` from today's class
- [ ] **Navigation** between them (a simple nav in the layout is perfect)
- [ ] At least **one component** doing honest work (`PullQuote`, `Byline`, or one of your own invention)
- [ ] **Images pass the audit**: loading *on Netlify* (not just localhost), lowercase filenames, compressed, alt text
- [ ] **Continuous deployment on**: you push, Netlify rebuilds. If you connected it in class today, shipping tonight is literally just committing.
- [ ] The URL is renamed to something nice (`yourname-letters.netlify.app`, not `stoic-panini-4f2a91`)

## Submitting

Head to the submission form via the 'Submit' button on the sidebar. Enter:

- The letter you chose (writer + recipient)
- Your name
- The live URL

Click 'Create'. All our letters get compiled into a single anthology site, a collective bookshelf of correspondence across two centuries, typeset by this class. You'll be able to send one URL to anyone and say *"we made that."*

## A note on doneness

The bar is "would the letter-writer forgive the medium", not perfection. If your dropcap is stubborn or a margin misbehaves, ship anyway and note it in your dev notes. Tomorrow's critique is where good pages become better ones, and an unfinished page that's live tonight is worth more than a perfect one sitting on localhost.
