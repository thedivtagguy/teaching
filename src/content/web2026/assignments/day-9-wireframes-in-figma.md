# Wireframes in Figma

Using what our guest taught this afternoon: **mid-fidelity wireframes for two pages of your personal site**, in Figma. Due at the start of Day 10. **Time-box: 90 minutes.**

## The rules

1. **Real content only.** Pull the actual words from your reply letter and your plots. Every box in your wireframe should contain (or clearly stand for) something that exists. Lorem ipsum is how layouts lie to you: a layout that only works with fake text doesn't work.
2. **Mid-fi means**: real text hierarchy (big/medium/small), gray boxes for images, actual navigation labels. No colors, no fonts, no styling seductions yet; that's tomorrow, when your design tokens arrive.
3. **Hierarchy first.** Before you draw, decide: what's the one thing each page most wants a visitor to see? Give it the space that says so.
4. **Two pages**: your homepage, plus whichever of your three plots feels most alive.

## The self-check (before you call it done)

- **Squint test**: zoom out or blur your eyes. Does the important thing still dominate? If everything is the same size, nothing is important.
- **The stranger test**: would the person your reply letter is addressed to know where to click first?
- **The honesty test**: is there any box you can't name the real content for? Delete it or fill it.

## Submitting

Post a screenshot (or Figma share link) in tonight's dev note, with one sentence: *"The first thing I want a visitor to see is ___."* If your wireframe doesn't make that sentence obviously true, that's your revision note for the morning.

Tomorrow: tokens, and then your site goes live before it has any design at all, so sleep well.
