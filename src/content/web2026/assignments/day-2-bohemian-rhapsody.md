We started this in class: take the [Bohemian Rhapsody starter repository](https://github.com/open-making/web2026-bohemian-rhapsody), and make it as visually offensive as possible. Clashing colors, cursed fonts, broken layouts; anything goes. The goal is to learn CSS properties through experimentation rather than trying to follow design rules. Perfectionism is structurally impossible on this assignment, which is the point.

**Due at the start of Day 4's class. You have two evenings; time-box each at 90 minutes.** If you're stuck mid-crime, post a stuck-note dev note and stop; that counts.

The one rule: **you have to find out how to commit each crime yourself.** Want blinking text? Rainbow borders? The opera section rotated 3 degrees for no reason? Ask questions like "How do I change the font?" or "How do I add spacing between paragraphs?", then search, or ask an LLM ([the right way](/web2026/ai-policy)). Web development is 50% coding, 50% looking things up and knowing how to find answers. That second skill is what this assignment actually teaches.

When you're satisfied with your monstrosity:

1. **Commit and push it** with GitHub Desktop (the save-game ritual from class)
2. **Deploy it to Netlify** the way we did together
3. **Share the live URL** on the submission thread; we want to see your beautiful disaster

On Day 4 we'll do a quick "ugliest in show" tour. There are no losers, only different flavors of winning.
