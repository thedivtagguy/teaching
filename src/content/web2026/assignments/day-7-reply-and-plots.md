# Your Reply, and Your Plots

Tonight's homework is writing, not code. **In your own voice: no LLMs**, per the [AI policy](/web2026/ai-policy). Due at the start of Day 8.

## Part 1: Finish your reply letter (≈45 min)

You started this in class today: a letter to a stranger who finds your website. Finish it tonight. 200 to 500 words is plenty.

If you're staring at a blank page, answer any two of these and you'll find your letter has started itself:

- Who do you *hope* wanders into your site? Describe them.
- What's one thing you make, collect, or care about that you'd want them to see first?
- What did the letter-writer you typeset this week say that you'd pass along, or push back on?
- What do you want this space to be in one year?

Tomorrow this becomes `reply.astro`, the second page of your Letters site. Next week, you'll notice it quietly turning into your personal site's about page. That's the trick of this assignment: you will never again face a blank homepage, because you've already written to the person it's for.

## Part 2: Your three plots (≈15 min)

From today's brainstorm, commit to **three plots** for your digital garden, the sections your personal site will open with. For each plot, one line on what it holds and one line on how often you might tend it. Some examples to get you thinking: an art journal, a gratitude list, things-I'm-learning notes, playlists, a project shelf.

Three is a floor for ideas but a *ceiling* for the course: you can dream bigger in your manifesto, but your site next week builds **at most three** sections. If you try to build six, you will ship one; the cap is there to protect you from that math.

## Submitting

Post both (reply letter text and your three plots) as tonight's dev note. Reading each other's is encouraged and will be the best thing on the internet that evening.
