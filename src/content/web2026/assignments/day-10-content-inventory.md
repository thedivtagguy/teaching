# Content Inventory

Your site is live. Your tokens are in. Your backlog exists. The only thing standing between you and a great sprint week is the hard part nobody schedules: **the words**.

Tonight, write the real content for every page you planned. **No code.** Seriously, do not open VS Code. Due at the start of Day 11 (sprint day one). **Time-box: 90 minutes.**

## What to produce

A single document (Obsidian, Google Doc, paper, anything) containing, for each planned page:

- **Homepage**: your name/title line, your one-sentence introduction (steal it from your reply letter; it's in there, I promise), and the labels for your navigation.
- **Each plot (max three, remember)**: its title, a two or three sentence introduction, and the first *real entries*. An art journal needs its first two artworks picked and captioned. A learnings page needs three actual learnings. A playlist page needs one actual playlist.
- **About/manifesto page**: your reply letter, edited however you like, possibly with your manifesto woven in. This page is 80% written already; tonight you're an editor, not an author.

Bullet points are fine, and final prose is not required; the words just have to exist. "I'll write it when I build the page" is the lie that killed a hundred project weeks. During sprints, every hour spent inventing content is an hour not spent building, and content invented at 4pm under deadline reads like it.

## Why this works

The people who struggle most in project week are usually not fighting CSS; they're deciding *what to say* while trying to build the thing saying it. You've spent two weeks accidentally preparing: an archaeology moodboard (your taste), a typeset letter (your craft), a reply letter (your voice), three plots (your scope). Tonight just collects the harvest.

Post your inventory (or a link/screenshot of it) as tonight's dev note. Tomorrow at standup, your first sprint goal will be sitting in it, already written.
