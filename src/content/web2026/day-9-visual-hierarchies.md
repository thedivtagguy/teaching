## Day 9: Visual Hierarchies + Figma

Big day: the anthology ships, we learn to see hierarchy, and a guest shows us Figma properly.

**Warm-up (10 min):** _The squint test._ I'll put blurred screenshots of famous pages on the projector. All the detail is gone, so what survives? Whatever you can still see when squinting *is* the hierarchy, and if nothing survives the blur, the page never had one.

### Morning, part 1: The Letters Anthology 💌

All your two-page Letters sites are compiled into a single anthology: a collective bookshelf of typeset correspondence, live at one URL you can send to anyone ("HEY, I MADE THAT"). We'll do a **gallery walk critique**: two rounds, small groups, using three prompts.

1. *What does this page make you feel before you read a word?* (that's the typography working)
2. *Where do your eyes go first, second, third?* (that's hierarchy)
3. *One thing you'd steal for your own site.* (stealing is how design taste spreads)

Kind, specific, useful, in that order.

### Morning, part 2: Visual hierarchy & wireframes

Then we turn to your personal sites, and before we push any pixels, we sort out priority.

- Moving from content gathering (your plots, your manifesto) to visual organization: which elements deserve to be seen first?
- **Content-first wireframing**: write the actual words first, then draw boxes around them (lorem ipsum is how layouts lie to you).
- Low-fidelity mockups: boxes and outlines only. Strip away text styling, images, fonts, and colors to focus purely on layout hierarchy; if it doesn't work as gray boxes, no font will save it.

### Afternoon: Figma guest lecture 🎨

We're joined by a guest for a hands-on Figma session: frames, auto-layout, components (you'll notice the concept rhymes with what you built in Astro yesterday; that's not a coincidence), and how designers actually hand off ideas to code. You'll leave with a working file and enough Figma to design tomorrow's mockups in it.

Homework: **mid-fi wireframes of two pages of your personal site**, in Figma, using your real content. Run your own squint test before you call it done. Details on the [assignments page](/web2026/assignments). Time-box: 90 minutes.
