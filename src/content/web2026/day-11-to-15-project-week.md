# Project Week

From Friday, July 31 to Tuesday, August 4 we go heads-down on your websites, and on Wednesday, August 5 we show them to the world. Treat these days as if you were on contract to yourself: your client is you, your audience is also you and maybe a few friends you'd like to send to your website on showcase day.

You are not starting this week by learning tools. Your site is **already live**, your tokens are in, your content inventory is written, and your backlog exists, so this week is for making the thing. If it helps, think of it less as "finishing" and more as "shipping your draft." Getting something that works, that someone else can visit and click around, is already a big achievement, and everything after that is iteration and polish.

### Daily rituals

- **Standup (first 15 min):** one thing you'll solve or finish today. Small mountains only: "get my homepage markup written," not "finish absolutely everything completely in entirety oh no what do I do."
- **Bug of the day (after lunch, 10 min):** someone presents the day's best bug: what broke, what the error said, how it got fixed (or didn't yet). Some of the best learning in this course happens in collective debugging, so we're putting it on the schedule; presenting a bug is a flex, not a confession.
- **Dev notes (last 15 min):** log progress, problems, discoveries. "Here's the thing about Astro layouts I wish I'd known on Monday…" is gold, for you and for whoever takes this course after you.
- **Commit and push before you leave.** As always. Your site rebuilds itself; that's the muscle you built all course.

### The sprint arc

**Day 11 (Friday, Jul 31): Structure.** Page skeletons with your *real content* from the inventory. Semantic markup, navigation between pages, everything reachable by clicking. It can be as ugly as it likes for now, as long as nothing is empty.

**Day 12 (Saturday, Aug 1): Layout & style.** Your grids, your type, your components. Midday **peer-review pairs**: swap laptops, click through each other's sites, note where you got lost. We end early today and there is no homework; the schedule builds in rest on purpose, and renovation energy resumes Monday.

**Day 13 (Monday, Aug 3): Polish & responsive.** The detail pass: spacing, hover states, a narrow-screen check on every page. Plus the **image audit**, four questions for every image on your site, no exceptions: does it load *on Netlify* (not just localhost)? Is the filename lowercase and sensible? Is it under 500KB? Does it have alt text?

**Day 14 (Tuesday, Aug 4): Freeze & ship.** Midday **content freeze**: after lunch, no new sections, no new pages, only fixing what exists. Afternoon: cross-device testing (swap phones and laptops with a neighbor), a final squint test, close remaining issues or explicitly move them to a "someday" list. Ship by end of class; nobody all-nighters into the showcase, and the site you have at 5 PM is the site you show.

**Day 15 (Wednesday, Aug 5): Showcase 🎉** Presentations and a gallery walk. Each of you gets a few minutes: show your site, read one line from your reply letter, tell us one thing you can now do that you couldn't three weeks ago. The class gallery goes live and you can send it to absolutely everyone. Then: a final reflection dev note, a course retro, and a map of where to go next (JavaScript, accessibility, CMSs, creative coding, pick your adventure).

### Your goals by showcase

- **At least two actual web pages that feel like you**, not "assignments." Good enough that you'd consider shelling out a few hundred rupees for a domain name. We want everyone leaving wanting their own `.com` or `.in`.
- **Real content**: not bare-bones filler, but something you could see a friend or a kind stranger actually reading.
- **Live on Netlify** at your URL, images working, nav working.

If you're ever in doubt about what to do next, ask: *"Would I show this to someone next week and feel at least a little bit proud?"* If yes, ship it; if not, defer it and pick a smaller, steadier win instead.

### Mini-lesson menu

During sprints I'm on call, and any table can order a 15-minute mini-lesson when three or more people need the same thing:

- Responsive images (`srcset` without tears)
- Nav patterns (mobile menus that don't require JavaScript degrees)
- A simple contact/footer section
- "It works locally but not on Netlify" forensics
- Fancy type moments (dropcaps, pull quotes) revisited
- Astro odds and ends (one more component, one more layout)
