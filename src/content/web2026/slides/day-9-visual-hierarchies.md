## Gallery walk critique

Two rounds, small groups. Three prompts, in order:

1. **What does this page make you *feel* before you read a word?**
   *(that's the typography working)*

2. **Where do your eyes go first, second, third?**
   *(that's hierarchy)*

3. **One thing you'd steal for your own site.**
   *(stealing is how design taste spreads)*

Kind, specific, useful, in that order.

## Content-first wireframing

**Write the actual words. Then draw boxes around them.**

Lorem ipsum is how layouts lie to you: a layout that only works with fake text doesn't work.<!--element class="fragment"-->

Your reply letter is real words. Your plots are real sections. Use them.<!--element class="fragment"-->

---

## Low-fidelity means low-fidelity

Boxes and outlines. No fonts, no colors, no images, just gray rectangles.

If it doesn't work as gray boxes, no font will save it. Get the layout working before any styling arrives.

---

## The 10-second test

Someone lands on your homepage for the first time. In 10 seconds they need:

- Who you are
- What you care about
- Why they should stick around

Yesterday's map of your landscape → today's boxes in an order.

---

## Sketch round (30 min)

Paper first. Three different arrangements of your homepage. Yes, three: the first idea is rarely the best one.

Then the squint test on your own sketches.
