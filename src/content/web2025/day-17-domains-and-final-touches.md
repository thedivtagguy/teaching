# Day 17: Domains and Final Touches

We're already at the end! Good lord, it has been a ride.

Today is our last working day before we put up our work for people to see tomorrow and for us to take it easy and step back from VS Code. I would like _everyone_ to be present tomorrow, with the following things ready for the exhibition: 

This module began with a metaphor that would guide all activities that were to follow (and, as we will describe later, only one condition). This was the metaphor of the web as a physical space.

The internet today has become boring and more or less the same. If it were to be thought of as 

garden
bookshelf
city

we'd much rather have it be the more organic, interesting, personalized and welcoming kind. Not for optimizing user 'conversions' and sales, or thinking in terms of sterile 'user-centricity' but a space that reflected and accommodated _us_ and our tastes.

To do this effectively, we needed to draw ourselves out of Behance, Dribbble, Pinterest and all of these places and look back at the early eras of the web. We set out to be digital archaeologists, roaming the ruins of Geocities and exploring the Internet Archive, collecting interesting things we found along the way.

Right away on Day 2, we jumped into getting introduced to HTML and CSS! Before we even <em>thought</em> about designing things that looked "nice" and worthy of being made by designers, it felt like a better idea to get our hands and brains a little messy. This exercise was similar to splashing paint on walls or doodling on the back of notebooks; except we needed to get our sites to look as ugly as possible with any new CSS properties we could discover. Starting with a plain, unstyled HTML document, we proceeded to making the biggest mess we could manage in an afternoon. Our sites were up on the internet from the second day too!
